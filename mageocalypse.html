Mageocalypse

Create spells and change everything.

A TTRPG about creating spells and unleashing the unthinkable. There is no spell list in Mageocalypse — magic is built, not assigned, and every mage at the table constructs their own from first principles.

The system is deliberately genre-agnostic: the same rules power a far-future colony, a present-day thriller, or a high-fantasy kingdom. Wherever there are mages, there is a Mageocalypse waiting to happen.

The Core Idea

You Are the Spell List

Element+ Method+ Critical Effect= Your Spell

Every attack spell is assembled from three choices: an Element (ten core schools, with four advanced ones — Psychic, Blood, Time, Illusion — unlocking as you grow), a Method of delivery, and a Critical Effect that fires when the dice sing.

Utility magic runs on its own track — non-elemental spells drawing from a shared daily pool — so the builder covers the whole art, not just the artillery.

Delivery & Defense

Twelve Ways to Say It

Methods are how a spell reaches the world — from close-quarters Blades and Touch through ranged Bolts and Lances to the wide ruin of Bursts, Storms, and Sigils.

Every mage chooses one permanent Method at the very start — Bolt, Blade, Greatweapon, or Touch — the one delivery that never changes, no matter how often the rest of you does. Choose how you reach the world before you choose what you do to it.

Beyond that, Methods split into Selective (choose your targets) and Indiscriminate (hit everything, and live with it). Storms don't care who your friends are.

Four Methods are shields instead of swords: Ward, Aura, Conjure, and Bind — and each one crosses with every element to produce a distinct defense.

A Fire Ward burns the attacker who dares melee you. A Time Bind is one full turn of complete suspension. Your defense carries your identity as loudly as your attack.

Growth

Never Stuck. Never Finished.

The Epiphany

Every level grants a respec — reshape any pick, unlock advanced elements from level 2, and at level 6 rebuild your entire kit. A mage who picks "wrong" at level one is never stuck. Only your permanent Method — the Bolt, Blade, Greatweapon, or Touch you began with — is forever.

Fusion Spells

The signature of a master: fuse two known elements into a spell neither produces alone. All 91 two-element pairings are fully realized — from Plasma Lance to Eclipse — rolling two damage dice and answering to no table but their own.

Five Stats, One Die

Power, Focus, Resilience, Attunement, Swiftness. Combat is d20 + Focus against a flat dodge threshold — Focus widens your crit range, Attunement stretches your durations, Swiftness decides who moves first.

Crits That Mean It

A critical hit fires your chosen Critical Effect — the third term of your formula. A natural 1 rolls the d12 fumble table, because unthinkable power has unthinkable Mondays.

Ten Levels of Ladder

From Apprentice to Archmage Supreme — each rung adding elements, methods, effects, and utility slots to the machine you're building.

Any World You Point It At

A tier-based, genre-agnostic creature builder with an ability menu and encounter budget means the Propagator of any setting can field foes worth melting.

The Rulebook

Complete, Audited, Nearly Ready

Mageocalypse ships as a full System Reference & Rulebook — sixteen chapters and two appendices in a classic two-column grimoire layout, with a printable character sheet.

  • The complete builder: all four tables — Element, Method, Critical Effect, Utility — plus every Defensive Intersection.
  • Character creation with standard array or point buy, leveling from Apprentice to Archmage Supreme, and the optional Paths.
  • The full 91-entry Fusion catalogue — every two-element pairing designed, named, and statted.
  • Combat, healing, death, and non-combat play, a quick reference, a condition glossary, and the genre-agnostic creature builder.

The manuscript has been through a heavy multi-pass quality audit; what remains before release is final balance sign-off and polish.
