The Long Road

A whole campaign-ready RPG on a single sheet of paper.

One universal roll. Eight traits. Three marks. No setting in the box — because your table forges the world in the first twenty minutes, and then walks it for as long as you like.

The Engine

One Roll, Four Mile Markers

When the outcome is uncertain: roll 2d6 + your most relevant trait. A trait applies when your approach matches who your character is — not just what they're doing.

2–6

It Goes Wrong

Something bad happens, and it gets complicated.

7–9

Partial Success

You get what you want — at a cost.

10–12

Clean Success

Narrate how it goes right.

13+

Outstanding

Something unexpectedly good happens too.

Characters are three traits from a list of eight — Reckless, Careful, Clever, Forceful, Empathic, Relentless, Deceptive, Steady — plus an Anchor: one phrase you define yourself, always worth +1 when it clearly applies.

The Thesis

Scars Are How Characters Change

There are no hit points and no experience points — there are three marks, and they are both.

When you're hurt — physically or emotionally — take a mark and name it. At three, you're out of the scene. Physical marks heal with safe rest; emotional marks clear only when a bond is honored or a truth is accepted. And at the end of each session, every mark that genuinely changed your character buys growth: a raised trait, a rewritten anchor, a changed bond.

A session with no marks produces no growth. The road doesn't teach the untouched.

Every character carries three Bonds — people, places, or beliefs, one sentence each.

Once per session, call on a bond for +2 to any roll — and afterward, the bond must be tested. Something changes, or something gets complicated. Your greatest strengths are the things you can lose.

Harm, healing, and advancement are one loop: the same named scars that wound you are the ones that grow you.

Any Genre, Every Table

The World Forge

Session Zero in Twenty Minutes

Five guided questions build the setting at the table — "Who holds power here, and what would they do to keep it?" — and every campaign opens its sessions with fresh world questions.

GM Sparks

A d10 oracle for when a scene goes flat: a clock starts ticking, the plan no longer works, something assumed to be true isn't. Prep and improvisation, wired into one table.

Scene Questions

Each scene opens on a question that moves the world's trouble forward, and plays until it's answered. End with growth.

Real Endings

Campaigns end when the central tension resolves or collapses. Each player narrates their character's fate — then the table decides: same world, new trouble? Or build something new?

The Design Feat

Everything It Kept, Everything It Cut

One double-sided sheet: all the rules on the front, session setup and a copy-to-an-index-card character sheet on the back. Print it, fold it, play for a year.

  • Kept: the full success ladder, fiction-first trait matching, relationship bonds, emotional harm as real as physical, collaborative worldbuilding, and an actual campaign ending.
  • Cut: classes became eight traits and a self-defined anchor. Move lists became one roll. Hit points became three named marks. XP tables became the scars themselves.
