Salt & Sorrow

Hunt the Legends. Survive the Age.

A d100 Dark Age investigation game of moral weight and wyrd horror. The monsters of folklore are real — and every one of them is a problem to be solved, not a thing to be killed.

You are no hero. You are a survivor — a knight without land, a hedge witch, a freed serf, a disgraced monk — scraping meaning from a brutal world.

The Setting

The Sundered Lands

A between-time drawn from Anglo-Saxon, Norse, and Germanic tradition: the wreckage of an empire nobody alive remembers clearly. Roads lead to ruins. Laws cite authorities that no longer exist. Something ended, and what's next hasn't arrived.

Beneath it all runs the wyrd — the old, strange order that predates humanity. The sea has always been wyrd. The wood keeps what it keeps. And the two faiths wrestling for the age — the expanding White Path and the fading Old Way, which managed the wyrd world for generations — are both right about something. There is no correct side.

Gritty, but not grimdark. Dangerous, but not arbitrary. Morally complex, but never relativist.

The Signature Mechanic

One Track Carries Everything

Every brutal choice adds Moral Burden. Every brush with the uncanny adds Wyrd Burden. Both land on the same 0–10 track — and the track does not care which kind broke you.

0–2

Clear

You sleep. For now.

3–4

Weighted

It shows when you speak. People notice.

5–6

Haunted

The past starts choosing its moments.

7–8

Fractured

Something in you stops working right.

9

Breaking

Face what weighs on you, or freeze.

10

Broken

The road ends here — in this state, no further.

Burden doesn't grind off with rest. It reduces the way it was earned — confession and restitution, cleansing rites and wardings, finishing the hunt, or time and distance. Breaking a sworn Tie inflicts Moral Burden with no resistance roll allowed. Choose your oaths carefully.

Characters

Archetypes, Not Classes

Ten backgrounds — Warrior, Scout, Thief, Apothecary, Scholar, Hedge Witch, Preacher, Skald, Commander, Emissary — that shape where you start, never where you're allowed to go. Beneath them: six attributes, thirty-seven percentile skills, and the Ties that hold you to the world.

The System

The Percentile Engine

Roll d100 at or under your skill. Roll 01–05 and shine; miss by five or less and succeed at a cost; land 96–100 and learn what a fumble means in the Dark Ages.

Problems, Not Monsters

Nineteen folkloric creatures — Draugr, Huldra, the Wild Hunt, the Barrow Wight — each with signs, a human story, and a resolution that usually isn't a sword. Something called it. Something keeps it here. Finding that something is half the adventure.

Describe Signs, Not Monsters

The GM's craft is dread: hoofprints that end mid-field, milk that sours in a sealed churn, a village that stopped dreaming. You see the creature late, if at all.

The Momentum Pool

A shared party resource that carries between sessions — earned by brilliance, spent to connect the dots, turn a hit, or say not today to the dark.

Wounds, Not Hit Points

Unharmed, Wounded, Badly Wounded, down — a short ladder with real consequences at every rung. Combat is fast, ugly, and desperately dangerous. Investigate first.

Marks, Capped at Mortal

Skills grow through meaningful successes and daring failures alike — and hard-cap at 90%. Nobody gets to be invincible.

Ready to Run

The Long Sleep

A three-act campaign of eighteen adventures spanning all five regions, opening with a Heartlands village dying slowly — not from blade or plague, but from exhaustion, from dread, from something that settles on sleepers in the dark.

Before the White Path. Before the Old Way. Something held the world together. It is waking up. One man has decided he knows what to do about that. He is not wrong about the problem. He is not right about the solution. The difference is everyone.

Act One: Signs in the Ground. Act Two: The Pattern Emerges. Act Three: The Long Reckoning. Alongside it: a standalone one-shot, The Miller's Debt, plus a browser character sheet and GM screen. The v4 manuscript is text-complete; art and final layout are the road ahead.
