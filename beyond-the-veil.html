Beyond the Veil

Investigate. Communicate. Survive.

A roguelite ghost-hunting TTRPG for 1–4 players. Take the contract, sweep the site room by room, read the evidence — and contain what's haunting it before your health or your sanity gives out.

Every session is one investigation. Not every investigator comes back — but everything you earn, you keep. Some things are better left undisturbed. You disturb them for a living.

The Core Loop

One Session, One Investigation

1 · Gear Up→ 2 · Explore→ 3 · Investigate→ 4 · Confront→ 5 · Debrief

The site is a deck of rooms drawn from a d100 table — calm rooms, evidence, hazards, minor spirits, merchants, and the occasional surge. Every room you enter feeds the Haunting Meter. At 8, the boss manifests, ready or not.

The Door Choice: peek at two rooms, enter one, lose the other forever. The whole roguelite in a single decision.

Evidence comes in six flavors — Cold Spots, Shadow Figures, Spirit Voices, EMF Spikes, Moving Objects, Blood Writing — and three matching clues name your ghost. Feeling brave? Gamble on an Early Identification: one ritual, one roll. Guess right and the hunt tips your way. Guess wrong and the boss comes early.

Two ways to play, one table or none:

  • Solo: you know exactly what's in the house — the game is surviving your preparation meeting reality.
  • Group: one player runs the ghost; the rest deduce its identity from evidence, majority votes, and dwindling nerve.

Twenty ghosts wait in the compendium. Which one you've drawn — and what it wants — is the whole game.

The System

Fast Dice, Slow Dread

d20 + Stat, Flat DCs

Five stats — Combat, Awareness, Tech, Occult, Resolve. Two actions a turn from a menu of six. Positioning is theater-of-mind Near/Far: no grid, no slowdown.

Sanity Is a Second Health Bar

Ten Health, ten Sanity. At 5 the whispers start. At 3, hallucinations. At 1 you can barely act — and at 0 the ghost doesn't kill you. It keeps you.

Ghosts Don't Roll

You attack on 12+; ghosts deal fixed damage on their turn. The tension isn't whether it hits — it's how long you choose to stay in the room.

Contain, Don't Kill

Reduce the boss and bind it with one final Tech or Occult ritual. Fail, and it escapes the site — and escaped ghosts remember.

Gear Is a Loadout

Twelve devices, limited hands, one van. What you carry through the front door is a bet on what's inside.

Death Isn't Deletion

If you fall or break, the investigation fails — but the Ectoplasm comes home with you. Every run buys the next one.

The Roguelite Spine

Win or Lose, You Keep the Ectoplasm

  • HQ Upgrades: spend Ectoplasm on permanent improvements between runs — the base grows even when the hunters don't come home.
  • Temporary Boons: run-only blessings scavenged mid-hunt; death wipes them, victory banks their memory.
  • Ghost Evolution: every ghost that escapes containment returns stronger, tier by tier, with a grudge shaped like your team.
  • The Threat Meter: a one-way campaign clock — with Contracts to choose and Reputation to earn as the region learns your name.
Every failure feeds the campaign. The question isn't whether you'll lose a hunter. It's what the survivors do about it.
