Mageocalypse

Coming Soon

A TTRPG about creating spells and unleashing the unthinkable. In Mageocalypse, magic is built, not assigned — there is no spell list, because you are the spell list.

Element + Method + Critical Effect = your spell. The intersection of your choices is your identity.The core formula — two mages at the same table might share zero spells in common.
  • Ten elements, plus four advanced schools unlocked as you grow — combined with twelve delivery Methods, from the humble Bolt to Storms, Sigils, and Wards.
  • The Epiphany: every level lets you reshape your magic. A mage who picks "wrong" at level one is never stuck.
  • Fusion Spells — the signature of a master: fuse two elements into something neither produces alone. All 91 pairings, fully realized.
  • Twelve optional Paths — from Battle Medic to Summoner to Trapper — and a genre-agnostic creature builder that works in any world you point it at.

Rulebook complete and rigorously audited · final polish underway.

Beyond the Veil

In Testing · v0.9.3

A roguelite ghost-hunting TTRPG for 1–4 players. Investigate. Communicate. Survive. Every session is one investigation — and not every investigator comes back.

  • One run, five phases: Gear Up → Explore → Investigate → Confront → Debrief. Sweep the site room by room while the Haunting Meter climbs — at 8, the boss manifests.
  • The Door Choice: peek at two rooms, enter one, lose the other. The whole roguelite in a single decision.
  • Sanity is a second health bar. Whispers at 5, hallucinations at 3 — and at zero, the ghost doesn't kill you. It keeps you.
  • Six hunters: Investigator, Enforcer, Tech Specialist, Exorcist, Medium, and the stubbornly ordinary Rook.
  • You don't kill it — you contain it. Fail, and it escapes to return stronger. Win or lose, you keep the Ectoplasm: upgrade the HQ, take the next contract.

Solo mode is a survival gauntlet; group mode hides the ghost's identity behind evidence your table must deduce together.

Crimson Depths

In Development · Playable Descent

A first-person, grid-based roguelite dungeon crawler in hand-drawn greyscale pixel art. You wake with no memory before a tree that should not exist, take a knife from the well inside it — and the only way out is down.

Greyscale means color is loud. The world is grey; red is reserved for the things that matter — exits, danger, blood, the supernatural.Design pillar
  • Read the telegraph, punish the opening. Every enemy shows its next move. Guard the heavy swing, strike when it's Exposed — or spend one of three precious bullets to interrupt.
  • One continuous descent through three hand-authored biomes — the Decrepit Dungeon, the Drowned Marsh, the Cold Forge — each with its own bestiary and a two-phase boss.
  • Scarcity is the horror. The knife is forever; everything else dwindles. No health regeneration — only what you carry.
  • The tree remembers, you don't. Death returns you to the well, and each death changes the tree. Progress is read in the world, not in menus.
  • The Mirror — bank red Sap between runs and barter it for permanence: Thicker Skin, Honed Edge, Deeper Chambers, an Ember in the Chest.

The Immoral

Concept · Early Prototype

A crude-humor parody of the late-'80s isometric dungeon crawler. The name says it all — it was supposed to read IMMORTAL, but the T snapped off, and nobody filed the repair form correctly.

You are an old, unscrupulous, thoroughly unbathed wizard descending through a dungeon staffed by unionized undead bureaucrats. The lich has a middle manager. There is a form for dying, and you filled it out wrong.

Death is not the end. It is, however, the start of paperwork.
  • Combat as puzzle: every foe is a telegraphed hazard with a named counter — parry in the pocket, dodge through the blade, and never, ever slash the Slime of Questionable Origin.
  • Hand-authored rooms with intent: "Lever Me Alone," "Grease Is the Word," "The Spike Metronome," "Rune Sudoku for the Damned."
  • Moral Debris — the meta-currency you bank by dying stupidly, spent in the Sanctum on upgrades like Thicker Skin.
  • Synergy chains: Grease Lightning + Greased Heels = the fabled Slip-'n-Sizzle. Skeletons in the Closet remains a legally distinct spell.

The hero sprite is locked and walks, fights, and dies in-engine today. Concept art shows the target look; the dungeon around him is still earning its textures.

Concept · Early Prototype
