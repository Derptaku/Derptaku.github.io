Glitchrix

Enter the Code. Write Your Legend.

A d20 tabletop RPG set on a world that looks like a high-fantasy epic — and hides a machine beneath its skin. The planet Glitchrix α was built from nanomachines: trillions of them, assembled into mountains, forests, rivers, and civilization.

The machine chose to wear the face of a fantasy world, and it wears it beautifully. Runes glow on every surface. The rivers run coolant. And death isn't the end here — it's a reset. Reset too many times, though, and the corruption might take hold.

The Setting

A World That Wears a Mask

Glitchrix α is a computer the size of a world, powered by a core called the Lumen Heart — and every inch of it plays fantasy. Faint blue-white leylines thread the soil, the tree bark, the castle mortar: the planet's circuitry, doubling as its roads and its resting places.

You are a Nano-Soul — a free-willed consciousness in a self-repairing body of living code. When you die, the leylines carry you home and the world gives you a new body. Unless you die near the Deadzone — the blacked-out expanse where the leylines have gone dark, coolant turns black and acidic, and a freed soul doesn't come home at all. It comes back as something else.

Like walking from a living body into a dead one — and every enemy was once a person.The Deadzone, which has already swallowed half a continent

This is why the world forged its fourteen Operating Systems: warriors built to fight what its automated defenses cannot.

Kernel

The central continent and beating heart of civilization — Hex Junction, the fortress Firewall, and the capital on its western shore.

Cache

The frozen north — Coldboot, Frostline, and the Howling Span across the Frozen Lattice.

Firmware

Tropics fading to desert — the Rust Flats, the Bloom, and a tower called the Arcade that nobody has ever finished climbing.

Peripheral

The eastern front line, half-consumed. Its border with the dark is called the Dimline, and the Dimline moves.

The Liquid Expanse

The coolant ocean — sixty percent of the surface, dotted with the Drift Isles.

The Deadzone

Where the world went dark. It started at Ground Zero a thousand years ago. It has not stopped.

The Capital

Mainframe

The greatest city on the planet sits on Kernel's western shore, atop the largest leyline nexus in existence — nine districts of neon-lit fantasy, from the Hall of Administrators down to the Red LED District.

Take a job from the Firewalls, spend your Octets at the Open-Source Market, catch a fight at the Sandbox Arena, and sleep it off at the Reboot Inn. Below the streets run the Recycle Bin Tunnels; on the eastern wall stands the sealed Null Gate, facing the Deadzone — and the fourteen OS Ambassador Buildings, one for every Operating System, recruit the warriors who keep it sealed.

The System

A d20 That Runs on Un-Debugged Code

Attacks roll over. Skills roll under. Your defense re-rolls itself every round — and the dice can literally glitch, because the world was never debugged.

Bugs & Glitches

A natural 1 triggers a Bug — misfires, feedback, friendly fire. A natural 20 triggers a Glitch — amplified damage, bonus actions. The tables exist because the world's code shipped untested.

Processing Speed

No static armor class: your Avoidance adds a die you re-roll every round. Some rounds you're untouchable. Some rounds you lag.

XP From Failure

Every failed roll earns experience. The system rewards trying, falling down, and standing back up — leveling is literally learning.

Soft & Hard Resets

Rest by a leyline to restore yourself — but stack quick Soft Resets without a full one and your maximum health shaves down, 25% at a time. Corruption creeps in through shortcuts.

Hacks & Subroutines

Active abilities spend Supply Power; passives run always-on. Health is Data Points, coin is Octets, and your gear carries up to three Mods.

Downed, Then Deleted

At zero DP you have three turns before permanent Deletion. Hex-grid combat with cover, facing, and flanking — fast, tactical, and personal.

Testing & Balance

One Book, Fully Written

The Glitchrix Master Rulebook is a complete manuscript — every rule, every class, every corner of the world already on the page.

  • Character creation with six stats, three generation methods, and Persona Traits — claws, wings, scales, whatever your code grew.
  • All fourteen Operating Systems statted from level 1 to 10, with full hack and subroutine lists.
  • A ~65-creature bestiary with scaling rules and a build-your-own-creature system — from the Fragmented to dragons to the Corrupted Soulreaver.
  • A full Game Developer's toolkit: encounter budgets, NPC and loot generators, biome encounter tables, travel rules — plus the starter adventure Signal in the Static.
  • The Arcade — a mysterious tower that works as a recurring dungeon or an entire level-1-to-endgame campaign on its own.

What Testing & Balance means here: the rules are mechanically complete; the dressing isn't. Art, maps, class emblems, and the printable character-sheet companion are the road ahead — and every playtest sharpens the numbers.
