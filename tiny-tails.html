Tiny Tails

Roll Small. Dream Big.

A tabletop RPG of tiny heroes and huge adventures. Small creatures. Real dangers. A suburban world that is enormous, political, and occasionally heartbreaking — seen from two inches off the ground.

It sits at an intersection two games rarely share: Disney warmth and genuine street-level stakes. Bright, funny, full of personality — and when someone goes down, it matters.

The Setting

Two Inches Off the Ground

The first principle of Tiny Tails: what is mundane to a human is epic to a mouse. That is the whole point.

A kitchen is
A Continent
A house cat is
A Dragon
A storm drain is
A Highway
A thunderstorm is
A Catastrophe

Beneath the humans' notice — they go to work, they come home, they don't look at the baseboards — the animals run a whole society in the gaps and margins: economies of barter and favor, territorial politics, nests that function as neighborhoods, and black markets convened under dumpsters.

There is no money. Information, food, trinkets, passage, favors, protection — the same item can be worth everything in one negotiation and nothing in the next.

Every animal shares a common tongue — a mouse can negotiate with a crow, plead with a cat, or cut a deal with a ferret syndicate. Humans hear only noise, which is a running source of both humor and pathos.

And predators remember what they are. A helpful cat is always performing a kind of restraint — and everyone at the table knows it.

The System

Roll Under. Fight Desperate.

Roll a d20 at or under your stat and you succeed — a natural 1 is a critical, a natural 20 is a disaster. That's the whole resolution system. Six stats, and everything else grows from them.

The Outcome Ladder

One d20 folds everything in: crit low, hit clean, land exactly on their Defense Rating for a graze, and fumble at the top. No second rolls, no math stack.

Momentum & Maneuvers

Combat builds a spendable rhythm — earn Momentum on hits, on hurts, and on crits; spend it on Feints, Shoves, Rallies, and Combo Strikes.

Scars That Stay

Hit zero and survive, and you're changed — Physical, Psychological, Quirky… or a Silver Lining. Some scars are secretly gifts. All of them are yours.

Trinkets Are Magic

The game's wonder is human technology no rodent understands — equipped, charged, and consumable relics, with an Ancient tier that only play can unlock.

No XP — Milestones

The Nestkeeper carries the party through five named tiers: Scurrier, Wanderer, Wayfarer, Pathmaker, Storyborn. Every tier, you Grow, Harden, Sharpen, or Strike.

The Nestkeeper Holds the World

Fiction-first initiative, a four-rung threat ladder from Minion to Legend, and a barter framework for a world with no coins in it.

Campaign I — Five Adventures, Every Tier

A complete campaign from Scurrier to Storyborn, played out across the gardens, walls, and rooftops of one very ordinary, very enormous neighborhood.

  • The Biscuit Job — Broccoli the corgi, the Short King of six suburban blocks, hires you to slip into a sealed garage and rescue a kidnapped elderly Shih Tzu.
  • What Morrow Keeps — a cat crime-boss calls in a debt: enter an occupied human home and retrieve a small brass cylinder from an operative who won't be needing it.
  • The Hollow Ward — the ferret syndicate known as the Business pulls you into a race through an abandoned hospital… and makes an offer.
  • The Weight of Five — three factions converge on a factory roof, a hawk's nest, and the first real choice about who the prize should serve.
  • What Was Left Behind — on the night everything commits, you descend below the factory, where something has been waiting a very long time.

The Players Guide runs ten chapters — rules, species, paths, weapons, trinkets, the world, and narrative companions (any hero can befriend a sparrow, a loyal dog, or an ancient garden tortoise — no stats, pure story). Writing and rules read finished; illustration is the road ahead.
